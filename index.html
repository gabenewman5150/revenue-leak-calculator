Why this isn't pulled out of thin air

Every number below is grounded in published industry data — local pack CTR studies from First Page Sage, conversion benchmarks from Ruler Analytics and Lucky Orange, and review-impact research from BrightLocal. The model is directional, not a promise — it tells you the order of magnitude of what you're leaking, not the exact dollar amount.

Potential at top-3 = views × 16.2% (avg of pos 1–3) × 0.15. The gap × your customer value = leaked revenue. Note: top-3 of the local pack is the threshold — the curve cliffs hard at position 4.

LEAK 2 · REPUTATION

Three signals roll into a single drag coefficient:

  • Star rating gap — distance from the 4.7 threshold (BrightLocal: ratings under 4.5 see meaningful click loss). Weighted 40%.
  • Review volume gap — distance from a 100-review benchmark. Weighted 35%.
  • Response rate gap — distance from an 80% response target. Weighted 25%.

Drag is applied to your existing GBP click volume × customer value × 0.6 dampening. The 0.6 prevents double-counting, since reputation also indirectly affects rank (which is already counted in Leak 1).

REVENUE LEAK SCORE

Composite of all three leak scores: (GBP × 0.35) + (Reputation × 0.30) + (Conversion × 0.35). GBP and conversion are weighted slightly higher because they sit at the top and bottom of the funnel — fixing them moves the most volume.

Caveats worth naming: This model assumes traffic and intake stay constant. It doesn't account for seasonality, paid search overlap, or branded vs. non-branded traffic mix. The 0.15 lead-to-customer rate on GBP clicks is a generic average — for high-consideration purchases (legal, real estate) it'll be lower, for transactional services (HVAC emergency calls) it'll be higher. Use the output as a directional signal, not a forecast.
